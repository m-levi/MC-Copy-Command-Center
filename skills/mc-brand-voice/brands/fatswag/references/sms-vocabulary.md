# FatSwag — SMS Vocabulary Reference

This vocabulary is pulled directly from Swag's 25 SMS campaigns (Dec 2024 – Mar 2026). Use these words naturally in email copy. Do NOT use words that aren't on this list just because they "sound streetwear."

---

## Swag's Actual Vocabulary

**Drop announcements:**
- "NEW DROP JUST HIT" — standard drop announcement
- "Drop day" — simple label
- "Just hit the site" — it's live

**Action words:**
- "Cop yours" — buy/get it
- "Tap in" — check it out / get involved
- "Peep" — look at / check out
- "Say less" — understood, no explanation needed
- "Don't sleep on" — don't miss out

**Urgency / scarcity:**
- "Very limited" — scarcity
- "This one will not sit" — it'll sell out
- "Before they gone" — scarcity + urgency
- "This is not a drill" / "This is not a fire drill" — real urgency
- "1 HOUR ONLY" / "for the next 30 minutes" — time-boxed

**Shipping / readiness:**
- "Premade" — in stock, not made-to-order
- "Ready to ship" — ships fast
- "NEXT DAY SHIPPING" — speed emphasis
- "Premade & shipping immediately" — combo

**Openers:**
- "Wyd??" — conversational, like a real text
- "Wake up" — attention grab
- "RESTOCK ALERT" — standard restock opener

**Community / hype:**
- "For the people" — community framing
- "Heavy fit" / "Hvy fit" — strong piece
- "New szn" — new season (abbreviated)
- "WHOLE WEBSITE" — his way of saying sitewide
- Collection names as headlines: "THE PINK EYE COLLECTION," "IM SO HOOD COLLECTION," "John Cena Collection"

**Punctuation patterns:**
- Double exclamation: "!!" and "!!!" for emphasis
- Double question marks: "??" for conversational energy
- ALL CAPS for the most important words (strategic, not everything)

---

## Words Swag Does NOT Use — Do Not Invent These

**Copywriter positioning language:**
- "Real streetwear" / "Real fit" / "Real quality"
- "No compromise"
- "Made for us" / "Built for us" / "Built different"
- "Streetwear for every size"

**Competitive framing:**
- "Nobody else is doing this"
- "Try finding this somewhere else"
- "The only brand that..."

**Generic marketing:**
- "Game-changer" / "Must-have" / "Essential"
- "Elevate" / "Curated" / "Designed to"
- "Inclusive fashion" / "Size-inclusive"
- "Fashion-forward" / "On-trend" / "Stylish"
- "We're so excited to share..."
- "Introducing our newest..."

**Polished brand-deck language:**
- "Same mission"
- "Our commitment to..."
- "We believe that..."
- "Fashion should have no limits"

---

## The Test

Before using a word or phrase in FatSwag copy, ask: **Would Swag text this to his audience?**

If the answer is no — if it sounds like a copywriter, a brand deck, an About page, or a marketing team — don't use it. Find the version Swag would actually say, or cut the line entirely.

Email has more room than SMS, but the vocabulary stays the same. More space means more visuals and layout — not more marketing language.
