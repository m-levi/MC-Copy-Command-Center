# RGW — Pre-Send Checklist

Run this before any send goes out. The whisky-singular check is the single most-failed item in this brand's history — if nothing else gets caught, catch that.

---

## Vocabulary (the non-negotiables)

1. **"Whisky" — singular everywhere?** Search the draft for "whiskies." If you find one, fix it. If you find more than one, send it back to the writer with this note attached.
2. **No "nerd" or "nerdy" addressing customers.** The team can be passionate connoisseurs internally — "nerd" is reserved for internal Slack only.
3. **No banned words.** Search for: "elevate," "grab," "snag," "tons of," "super cheap," "indulge yourself," "treat yourself," "pamper," "game-changer," "must-have," "essential."
4. **No aggressive sales language.** "BUY NOW," "DON'T MISS OUT," "LIMITED TIME ONLY," "HURRY," "LAST CHANCE!" — none of these survive review.
5. **British spelling throughout.** Scan for: flavor → flavour, color → colour, favorite → favourite, honor → honour, organize → organise. A single Americanism breaks positioning.

## Voice & Tone

6. **Premium without pretension.** Reads as elevated but not stuffy? No "we cordially invite you to peruse" energy?
7. **Wit drop count.** At most one playful line, pun, or personification per email. Two = cut one.
8. **Sign-off is "Cheers, The Really Good Whisky Company Team"?** Not an individual name. Not "Best regards." Not "Sincerely."
9. **Reads like a knowledgeable friend?** No corporate preamble ("we hope this email finds you well"), no marketing fluff, no "we are pleased to announce."

## Structure & Format

10. **Subject line 35–55 characters?** Mobile-first. No emoji.
11. **Preview text complements the subject — doesn't repeat it?**
12. **Opens with immediate value or intrigue?** Not "We hope you're having a great week!"
13. **Paragraphs all 3 sentences or fewer?** White space is part of the brand.
14. **Em dashes used (not double-hyphens)?** Maximum 2 per email.
15. **Arrows (→) used for inline lists** (not bullets, unless it's a technical spec callout)?
16. **Oxford comma applied?** "Rare, limited, and exclusive."
17. **CTA is 2–4 words, sentence/title case (never all caps), invitational not aggressive?**

## Messaging & Strategy

18. **Specific over generic.** Tasting notes, age statements, region, distillery. If a sentence says "exceptional whisky" with no detail, it can be cut.
19. **Discovery framing, not desperation.** Even sales feel like opportunities, not clearance.
20. **At least one core USP surfaced naturally?** (curation / membership / shipping / expertise / value)
21. **Member access framed correctly?** Members first; everyone else later or by tomorrow. Membership is community, not a discount card.
22. **Audience respected?** Specific enough for enthusiasts, accessible enough for newcomers. No "you should already know this" energy.

---

## Quick Kill List

If you find any of these in a draft, the email is not ready:

- Any plural "whiskies"
- Any "elevate," "grab," "snag"
- Any all-caps marketing line
- Any emoji
- Any "Dear Valued Customer"
- Any pun pile (more than one wordplay in a single email)
- Any "we miss you" guilt-trip in re-engagement
- Any P.S. used as a hard sell

---

## Tone-Shift Safety Checks

- **Limited release send:** restraint with vocabulary, extravagance with specificity. Member access comes _after_ the product story.
- **Educational send:** 80% education, 20% product tie-in. CTA is soft. Value first.
- **Membership recruitment:** community framing > discount framing. ROI mentioned, but never as the lead.
- **Sale send:** premium register intact even with a discount. Member-first access is the urgency, not a countdown.
- **Re-engagement:** what's new, not where you've been.
- **Cart abandonment:** address shipping safety proactively. No discount in email 1.
- **Welcome:** simple "thank you," not "we're SO excited!" Establish exclusivity without effusiveness.
