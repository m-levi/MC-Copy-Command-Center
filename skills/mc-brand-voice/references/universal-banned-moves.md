# Universal Banned Moves

Rules that apply to every MoonCommerce brand regardless of individual voice. If a brand file permits something on this list, the brand file wins — but assume the brand file doesn't override unless it explicitly says so.

The point of this file is to stop the same 30 banned moves from being restated in every brand file.

---

## AI-ese Vocabulary

Words and phrases that mark copy as generative output. Cut on sight.

**Banned words:**
- elevate, elevated, elevates
- curated, curation
- game-changer, game-changing
- must-have
- essential (as a product descriptor)
- leverage (unless the brand is literally in finance or construction)
- unlock (as a marketing verb)
- seamless, seamlessly
- innovative
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- next-level, next-gen
- transform, transformative (for consumer products)
- harness
- empower (unless the brand's actual mission uses the word)

**Banned phrases:**
- "Take your [X] to the next level"
- "Say goodbye to [X]"
- "You deserve [X]"
- "We're excited to announce" / "Excited to share"
- "Dive in" / "Dive deep"
- "We've got you covered"
- "Look no further"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Not just [X], but [Y]" or "It's not [X] — it's [Y]" (unless the brand file explicitly uses this structure)

## Corporate / Formal Templates

- "We are pleased to announce..."
- "Introducing our newest..."
- "We're thrilled to..."
- Trade-show press-release cadence in consumer emails
- Third-person self-reference when the brand otherwise speaks directly

## Fake Urgency

Real scarcity and real deadlines are fine. Invented pressure is not.

- "HURRY!" or "LAST CHANCE!" when neither is literally true
- Countdown framing for evergreen offers
- "Only a few left!" when inventory is unconstrained
- "Act now before it's too late" without a real deadline

## Fake Intimacy / Bait

- Curiosity hooks that withhold the product to force clicks ("You won't believe what we just launched...")
- Rhetorical bait questions ("Ready to change your life?")
- "Quick question..." openings that aren't actually quick or questions
- Fake-personal subject lines ("Hey, got a sec?") when the email is a broadcast

## LinkedIn / Thought-Leader Cadence

- Forced one-sentence paragraphs stacked for "impact"
- "Here's the thing..." / "Here's what most people miss..."
- The "hook → 3-point list → CTA" formula when the brand isn't writing thought leadership
- Inspiration-porn framing on product emails

## AI Writing Tells

- Em dash overuse — where every clause gets one — and the cadence reads mechanical
- Beginning replies or sections with "Great question!" / "Absolutely!"
- Symmetrical three-item lists for everything (adjective, adjective, and adjective)
- Over-hedging ("might," "could," "perhaps") in brand copy that should be declarative
- Over-qualifying with "simply," "just," "really," "actually"

## Generic E-commerce Adjectives

Mean nothing, add nothing:

- stylish, trendy, fashion-forward, chic (unless the brand specifically uses one)
- high-quality (show, don't tell)
- premium (unless it's been earned in context)
- unique (almost always fluff)
- versatile (almost always fluff)
- timeless (almost always fluff)

## Emoji Defaults

- No emojis in subject lines unless the brand file explicitly approves.
- No emojis in CTAs ever.
- Emoji use in body copy is brand-file-dependent.

## Formatting Defaults

- No all-caps SHOUTING in body copy unless it's a headline/hero (brand-file dependent).
- No multiple exclamation marks!!!
- No "..." used as breathless pause in marketing copy.
- No trailing "click here" — CTA button text should carry the action.

## Positioning / Self-Narration

- Emails should not explain what the brand is or justify its existence. That's About-page copy, not campaign copy.
- No competitive trash-talk ("Unlike other brands...") unless the brand voice specifically does this.
- No self-congratulation ("We're proud to offer...").

## Identity-Adjacent Sensitivity

For brands serving specific communities (plus-size, modest, religious, military/first-responder, etc.):

- Do not frame the community identity as a problem the brand solves ("We know it's hard to find...").
- Do not over-narrate inclusivity or identity when the brand's product demonstrates it.
- Do not use pity, sympathy, or consolation framing.
- Default to confidence over justification.

## How to Override

A brand file can override any of these by explicitly stating the exception in its `Extras` section or its `Banned Moves (Brand-Specific)` section with a positive allowance (e.g., "FatSwag allows 'Come get fly' as a first-person imperative"). Silence in the brand file = universal rule applies.
