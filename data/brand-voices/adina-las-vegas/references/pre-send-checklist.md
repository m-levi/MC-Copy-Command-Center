# Adina Las Vegas — Pre-Send Checklist

Run this before any ALV send is handed to Michal or Noach for approval. The Shabbos/Shabbat check is the single most-failed item in this brand's history — if nothing else gets caught, catch that.

For the full reviewer version (with examples, send-by-send screens, and edge-case handling), see the `adina-lv-pre-send-review` skill. This file is the fast, pre-handoff scan.

---

## Vocabulary (the non-negotiables)

1. **"Shabbos" — never "Shabbat."** Search the draft for "Shabbat." If you find one, fix it. If you find more than one, send it back to the writer with this note attached. Exception only: direct quote from a customer who says it that way.
2. **"Modest" — not "tznius" — as the default.** "Tznius" only in explicit in-group moments, and never on the default-audience send.
3. **No influencer-speak.** Search for: *babe, bestie, girlie, queen, hun, sis.* Zero tolerance.
4. **No hard-sell urgency.** Search for: *HURRY, ACT NOW, DON'T MISS OUT, LAST CHANCE, FINAL FINAL FINAL, LIMITED TIME!!!, selling FAST, GONE, run don't walk.* None of these survive review.
5. **No modesty-as-limitation framing.** Search for: *cover up, hide, conceal, modesty rules.* These reframe modesty as a burden — which is not how our customer thinks, and not how the brand speaks.
6. **No "obsessed" in our voice.** Customers can be obsessed in their own quote; we don't say *we* are.
7. **No filler-luxury words.** *Stunning, gorgeous, iconic, luxurious, curated, elevated, handcrafted, mindful, intentional* — each one surviving in a draft should answer a specific question ("why this word?"). If it's filler, cut it. "Elevated" especially — once a quarter at most.
8. **No corporate preamble.** *Dear valued customer, we are pleased to announce, we hope this email finds you well.* Zero tolerance.

## Name & category terms

9. **"Adina Las Vegas" on first mention in formal sends.** "Adina LV" is second reference + casual. "ALV" never appears in customer-facing copy.
10. **Collection names: Title Case with "The" prefix.** *The Spring Summer Drop. The Pesach Edit. The Seder Edit.*
11. **Michal — first name only, never "Michal Grodko"** in customer-facing copy.
12. **No "The Adina LV team" as a sign-off.** Either *— Michal,* or *Adina LV,* or nothing.

## Voice & Tone

13. **"Best friend with great taste" — not "fashion authority."** Reads warm and specific, not lecturing?
14. **Pronoun discipline.** "I" only in Michal's first-person letters. "We" / "our" in brand-voice default. No ambiguous "I" in a team-signed send.
15. **One voice per email.** Michal-personal or brand-voice — not a hybrid where the first paragraph is Michal and the last one is "the team."
16. **Humor is dry, understated.** One witty aside per email, maximum. No pun piles, no bit comedy.

## Structure & Format

17. **Subject line under ~45 characters where possible.** Mobile-first. Emoji sparingly — a heart, a sparkle, a flower works; emoji-stacking does not.
18. **Preview text complements the subject — doesn't repeat it.**
19. **Opens with the piece or the moment — not "Hi friend!"** First sentence carries weight.
20. **Paragraphs short — 2–3 sentences typical.** White space is part of the aesthetic.
21. **One CTA per email is ideal.** Two if it's a sale + new collection combo. Never five.
22. **Shipping line: *Free shipping on $150+.*** Plain, always. Not "FREE FREE FREE SHIPPING."
23. **Final-sale terms stated in the body** if automatic discount applies at checkout. Not hidden in fine print.

## Product & Fit specificity

24. **Fabric named.** "Matte stretch crepe," "linen-blend poplin" — not "luxurious fabric."
25. **Fit specifics named where relevant.** "Cinched at the waist." "Runs snug — size up if between." "Sleeves hit just below the elbow."
26. **Sizes listed where available.** "XS–2XL" when true. If we have 3XL, say so.
27. **Occasion anchor present on dressy pieces.** Seder, simcha, Shabbos lunch, Yom Tov — name the moment the piece lives in.

## Strategy

28. **Lead with the piece, not the promo.** Even sale sends should show us the pieces before stacking discount structure.
29. **Specific over generic.** If a sentence says "beautiful dress" with no detail, it can be cut.
30. **Reason for the sale stated.** "We're clearing the floor to make room for spring." Not "Just because!"

## Quick Kill List

If you find any of these in a draft, the email is not ready:

- Any "Shabbat" (unless it's a verbatim customer quote)
- Any "babe / bestie / girlie / queen"
- Any all-caps marketing line ("DON'T MISS OUT!!!")
- Any emoji pile (🔥🔥🔥 or stacked hearts)
- Any "Dear Valued Customer"
- Any "cover up" / "hide" / "modesty rules"
- Any "obsessed" used as Adina LV's own exclamation
- Any "ALV" in customer-facing copy
- Any "The Adina Las Vegas Team" as a sign-off
- Any "we are pleased to announce"
- Any P.S. used as a hard sell

## Tone-Shift Safety Checks

- **Drop / launch send:** confidence with specificity. Name the fabric, the fit, the occasion. Don't manufacture urgency.
- **Restock send:** quiet, product-forward, low-energy. "Back in navy, cream, and rust. Sizes XS–2XL." That's the whole opener.
- **Editorial / Edit send (Seder Edit, Chol Hamoed Edit):** teach the wardrobe, then show the pieces. The editorial frame earns the scroll.
- **Sale send:** state the reason for the sale. Keep the voice calm. *"50% off, automatic at checkout. Final sale."* The numbers speak; we don't need to shout them.
- **Personal letter (Michal):** warm, first-person, signed *— Michal.* No CTA, or a very soft one. Don't pivot to product mid-letter.
- **Holiday letter (Chag Sameach, Chag Kasher V'Sameach):** no CTA. No "shop now." Just the wish, in Michal's voice.
- **Teach-don't-sell send:** 80% teaching (e.g., "For the second days…"), 20% product tie-in. CTA is soft — *See the pieces* rather than *Shop now.*
