# Adina Las Vegas — Lexicon

The working vocabulary for ALV copy. Hero words are the ones that sit naturally in Michal's voice. Forbidden words cheapen the brand, break the modest-but-not-preachy frame, or mark us as a different kind of company than we are.

When in doubt, read it out loud. If it sounds like a fashion influencer, it's wrong. If it sounds like Aritzia for modest women, you're close.

---

## 1. Hero words

Reach for these. They sit naturally in Michal's voice and Adina's copy.

- **Piece** — "this piece," "every piece." Not "item," not "garment." Keeps the product feeling considered rather than inventory.
- **Fit** — noun and verb. "The fit is cinched." "It fits like it was made for you." One of our most-used words, and deliberately so.
- **Drape** — how the fabric falls. Specific, tactile, hard to fake.
- **Silhouette** — shape of the piece on the body. Fashion-literate without being fussy.
- **Cinched** — almost always at the waist. A hero term, literally and figuratively.
- **Feminine** — describes the brand's aesthetic directly. Not "girly."
- **Polished** — what the customer feels in our pieces. Better than "put-together."
- **Effortless** — the promise. Pieces that look styled without the customer having to try.
- **Considered** — describes design intent. "Every hem, every seam is considered."
- **Made for** — "made for Shabbos," "made for the woman who…" A structural phrase we lean on.
- **Drop** — the word for a new release. "The spring drop." "Drop 2." "The next drop."
- **Restocked / back** — "back in navy," "restocked in XS and 2XL." Customers wait for this word.
- **Shabbos-ready** — category shorthand that earns its keep. Avoid "Shabbos-approved," "Shabbos-appropriate" — they sound rabbinic.
- **Everyday** — one word, adjective. "Everyday skirt." Not "every-day."
- **Community** — our customers. Used sparingly and earned — not as a synonym for "email list."

## 2. Forbidden words

These are banned in customer-facing copy. The reasons matter more than the list.

- **babe, bestie, queen, girlie, hun, sis** — influencer-speak. Wrong customer, wrong tone. ALV is not a TikTok brand.
- **obsessed** (as *our* exclamation — "we're obsessed!") — overused and flat. Customers can be obsessed in a quote. We don't say *we* are.
- **game-changing, life-changing, revolutionary, miracle, must-have** — we don't over-promise. A dress is a dress.
- **HURRY, ACT NOW, DON'T MISS OUT, LAST CHANCE, FINAL FINAL FINAL, LIMITED TIME!!!** — hard-sell urgency. Never. Scarcity, when real, speaks plainly.
- **lowest prices, clearance blowout, everything must go** — cheapens the brand, even on sale emails. "We're clearing the floor" is our version.
- **perfect for everyone, flatters every body, works on every woman** — nothing does. Credibility breaks the second we claim this.
- **cover up, hide, conceal, modesty rules** — frames modesty as limitation. We don't write about modesty that way. We dress the woman she wants to be, not what she's hiding.
- **Dear valued customer, we are pleased to announce, we hope this email finds you well** — corporate-speak. Never.
- **luxurious, elevated, curated, handcrafted, mindful, intentional** — technically correct for our positioning, but drained of meaning by overuse. Use with intention, not as default filler. "Elevated" especially — once a quarter at most.
- **stunning, gorgeous, iconic, fire** (as default adjectives) — generic fashion copy. Pick a specific word that describes the actual piece.
- **journey, experience** (as nouns for the shopping process) — consultant-speak.
- **tznius** (as the default word for "modest") — acceptable in specific in-group moments only. See §3.
- **frumpy, boxy, shapeless** — even as counter-framing, don't name what we're not by insulting what others are. Builds the brand by dragging someone else down, which isn't our posture.

## 3. Proper terms for the category

ALV sits across two audiences — Jewish observant women (primary) and modest-fashion-curious women (growing segment). The category language needs to read correctly to the first group and warmly (not alienating) to the second.

- **Modest / modesty** — default language. Universal. Works for both audiences.
- **Tznius / tnius** — in-group only, very sparingly. Specific community events, certain organic social moments. Not the default.
- **Shabbos** (not Shabbat) — primary, always. This is a house rule and it matters. Michal has been explicit: Shabbos.
- **Shabbat** — acceptable only in a direct quote from a customer who says it that way, or in a very specific inclusive-market segment. Not the default. Not once.
- **Yom Tov** — use when we mean Yom Tov. Don't flatten to "holiday." Use "holiday" only when we genuinely mean a calendar holiday (Mother's Day, Memorial Day sale copy, etc.).
- **Pesach / Pesach Edit / Seder** — preferred over "Passover" and "Seder dinner."
- **Sukkos** (not Sukkot).
- **Chol Hamoed** — the middle days. Use the term; our customer knows what it means.
- **Simcha / simchas** — acceptable and warm. "For your next simcha."
- **Vort, bar mitzvah, bris, sheva brachos** — use when contextually right (dressing-for guides, event edits).
- **Frum / observant / religious** — "observant" in broader-market copy. "Frum" OK in insider segments. "Religious" only in demographic-data or partner-facing contexts, never in warm copy.
- **Bump-friendly, nursing-friendly** — hyphenated. Specific, warm, answers a real question.
- **Wholesale / retail / consignment** — backend language only. Customer-facing says "shop online," "shop in-store," or names the specific store.

## 4. Name & trademark treatment

- **Brand name in body copy:** "Adina Las Vegas" on first mention in a formal send (homepage copy, press, paid ads, founder letters). "Adina LV" for second reference and casual contexts (SMS, recurring email footer, internal docs).
- **Never in customer-facing:** "ALV." "Adina Las Vegas Collections." "Adina's Collection" (when we mean the brand rather than literally Michal's picks).
- **Collection / line names:** Title Case with "The" prefix. *The Spring Summer Drop.* *The Armoire Collection.* *The Pesach Edit.* *The Seder Edit.*
- **Trademark symbols (™, ®):** Don't use in marketing copy. Legal handles where needed.

## 5. Founder & team voice

- **Michal** — first name only in customer-facing copy. Signed on letters, store announcements, founder notes. Never "Michal Grodko" outside of press or LinkedIn.
- **The team / we** — for brand-voice sends not in Michal's first person. Never "our team of experts" — just "we."
- **Never:** "The Adina LV team" as a signature. Clunky. Either "Michal," "Adina LV," or nothing.

## 6. Customer pronouns

- **How we address the customer:** **"You."** Always. Second person, direct. No "y'all," no "friend," no "ladies," no "girlie."
- **How we refer to ourselves:**
  - **"I" / "my"** — personal-letter mode only. Always Michal's first person. Never "I" in a generic brand-voice email — it creates a "which I?" problem.
  - **"We" / "our"** — brand-voice default. Represents the team and the brand.

## 7. Micro-copy defaults

Small surfaces where the wrong word quietly cheapens the brand. Use these exact phrasings where possible.

- **CTAs:** *Shop the drop. Shop the edit. Shop her fit. Get on the list. See what's new.* Avoid: *Buy now, Add to cart!!! Shop now hurry!*
- **Sale framing:** *We're clearing the floor.* / *50% off, automatic at checkout. Final sale.* Avoid: *Everything must go. Blowout.*
- **Back-in-stock:** *Back in [colors]. Sizes XS–2XL.* Avoid: *OMG it's back!!! Don't sleep on it.*
- **Shipping note:** *Free shipping on $150+.* Plain, always. Never "FREE FREE FREE SHIPPING."
- **Sign-off for Michal letters:** *— Michal* (em-dash, no "xoxo," no "love," no signature graphic in body).
