# Copy Samples — Kosher Casual

**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-20
**Status:** DRAFT — starter samples pending validation and re-annotation

Annotated exemplars of Kosher Casual's strongest sends. When writing new copy for Kosher Casual, read these first. Patterns here override abstract rules when the two conflict.

---

## Sample 1 — Shell top restock (campaign)

**Subject line:** "Back in stock: shell tops in 6 everyday colors"
**Preview text:** "The layering top that actually stays in place all day — finally back and ready to ship."

**Body:**
```
Our most popular shell tops are back in stock. These are the layering pieces that stay in place from morning carpool to evening errands.

Made with soft stretch Turkish fabric that moves with you without riding up. The fabric won't stretch out or lose its shape, wash after wash.

Available in 6 everyday colors that go with everything you already own. Layer under dresses, blouses, or wear on its own. Makes any outfit instantly modest.

Same quality you've come to trust. Same consistent sizing.

[Shop shell tops]

As always, questions about sizing? Reach out to our customer service team.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** Shop shell tops

**Why this works:**
Opens with the benefit, not the announcement. "Stays in place from morning carpool to evening errands" anchors the abstract claim in a real scene. The closing "same quality, same consistent sizing" reinforces the reliability promise without restating it as a slogan — the sentences *do* the work of the brand positioning.

**What a less-on-brand version would have done:**
Led with "New arrivals!" or "20% off shell tops — limited time." Either way, the first sentence would be about the brand instead of about the customer's day.

## Sample 2 — Product education (campaign)

**Subject line:** "Three simple ways to make any dress modest"
**Preview text:** "No need to replace your whole wardrobe — just add one versatile layering piece."

**Body:**
```
You don't need to replace your entire wardrobe to dress modestly. A good shell top solves most coverage challenges.

Three ways to use a shell top:

1. Under sleeveless or short-sleeve dresses
Layer a long-sleeve or 3/4 sleeve shell top underneath. Instant coverage for arms and neckline.

2. Under low necklines
A sleeveless shell top adds coverage without bulk. Works under blouses, sweaters, and casual tees.

3. To extend the length of tops that are too short
Wear a longer shell top under shorter tunics or sweaters. No more tugging or adjusting.

Our shell tops are designed specifically for layering. Made with soft stretch fabric that won't add bulk or ride up. Available in everyday colors that work with everything.

[Find your perfect shell top]

Questions about which style works best? We're here to help.

The Kosher Casual Team

P.S. All our shell tops stay in place all day — whether you're layering or wearing them on their own.
```

**CTA:** Find your perfect shell top

**Why this works:**
The email is genuinely useful even if she doesn't buy. The three tips teach a use pattern most customers haven't fully thought through, and the product pitch lands naturally because it's the obvious solution to the problem we just taught. The P.S. reinforces a specific benefit without breaking the educational frame.

## Sample 3 — Welcome (flow, email 1)

**Subject line:** "Welcome! Here's what makes us different"
**Preview text:** "Quality modest basics you can count on — for over 20 years."

**Body:**
```
Welcome to Kosher Casual. We're glad you're here.

For over 20 years, we've been making comfortable modest basics for women who want practical everyday clothing. Shell tops that stay in place. Skirts that don't lose their shape. Basics you can count on.

Why women trust us:

We don't change our products every season. When you find a shell top you love, you can buy it again next year. Same quality. Same fit. Always.

We make everything in our own facility in Turkey. That means quality Turkish fabrics and consistent sizing you can count on.

Our layering pieces work with your wardrobe. You don't need to replace everything you own — just add pieces that make modest dressing easier.

[Browse our basics]

Questions about sizing or fit? Just reply to this email. We're here to help.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** Browse our basics

**Why this works:**
Orients the subscriber fast — who we are, what we make, why we're reliable — without leaning on adjectives. The three differentiators are stated as facts, not claims ("we don't change our products every season"), so the reader doesn't feel pitched to. The close with "just reply" turns the welcome into an invitation, not a broadcast.

## Sample 4 — Sale / promo (campaign)

**Subject line:** "Stock up and save: 20% off all shell tops"
**Preview text:** "Same quality you love, better prices for a limited time."

**Body:**
```
Take 20% off all shell tops today through Thursday. Stock up on the basics you wear every week.

These are the layering tops that stay in place all day. Made with soft stretch Turkish fabric that won't ride up or lose its shape. Available in 6 everyday colors that go with everything.

Use code SHELL20 at checkout. Sale ends Thursday at midnight.

[Shop the sale]

As always, our shell tops are made with the same quality fabric and consistent sizing you've come to trust.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** Shop the sale

**Why this works:**
Frames the sale as "stock up" — a planning move, not a panic move. The deadline is stated plainly ("Thursday at midnight") without countdown theater. The benefit reminder in the middle does most of the work — if she wasn't going to buy at full price, the reasons she *would* are still here.

**What a less-on-brand version would have done:**
Stacked urgency words, used all caps on the discount percentage, added a fire emoji, or led with "HURRY — ENDS MIDNIGHT!" That version targets impulse; this one targets the regular customer.

## Sample 5 — Problem/solution product feature (campaign)

**Subject line:** "No more tugging: shell tops that actually stay put"
**Preview text:** "Finally — coverage that doesn't ride up or shift throughout the day."

**Body:**
```
Tired of constantly pulling down your shell top? We get it. A layering piece that rides up defeats the whole purpose.

Our shell tops are designed to stay exactly where you put them. From morning meetings to evening errands, no tugging or adjusting needed.

What makes them stay in place:

The soft stretch Turkish fabric has just enough grip to stay put without being tight or uncomfortable. The length is designed to tuck in easily and stay tucked. The neckline sits where it should all day long.

Whether you're reaching, bending, or just going about your day, these shell tops move with you without riding up.

[Shop stay-put shell tops]

Same quality fabric that looks fresh wash after wash. Same consistent sizing you can count on.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** Shop stay-put shell tops

**Why this works:**
Names the exact pain ("pulling down your shell top") in plain language the customer uses herself. The "what makes them stay in place" section explains the fabric without reaching for technical terms — "just enough grip" is craft-level detail delivered conversationally. The CTA echoes the subject line's promise.

## Sample 6 — Cart abandonment (flow, email 1)

**Subject line:** "Still thinking about your shell top?"
**Preview text:** "It's still in your cart — and we're here if you have questions."

**Body:**
```
You left a shell top in your cart. We wanted to check in — is there anything we can help with?

Our shell tops stay in place all day and work with everything in your wardrobe. Made with quality Turkish fabric that won't stretch out or lose its shape.

Questions about sizing? Our customer service team is here to help. We can walk you through our size guide and help you find the perfect fit.

[Complete your order]

Or reply to this email with any questions. We're here to help.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** Complete your order

**Why this works:**
Reads as customer service, not marketing. Assumes the reason she hasn't checked out is uncertainty, not distraction, and offers real help to resolve it. No discount in email 1 — the voice stays grounded and the incentive lever stays preserved for later in the flow.

**Subject line:** "Your everyday basics — now in fall colors"
**Preview text:** "Same comfortable shell tops and tees, plus new seasonal colors for your wardrobe."

**Body:**
```
We've added new fall colors to our shell tops and tees. Same quality Turkish fabric. Same reliable fit. Just more color options for your wardrobe.

New colors available now:
- Deep burgundy
- Forest green
- Warm taupe
- Charcoal gray

All our shell tops stay in place all day and work with everything you already own. The soft stretch fabric won't ride up or lose its shape, wash after wash.

[See all fall colors]

Your favorite styles are still available in all the classic colors you love. We just added a few more options.

The Kosher Casual Team
```

**CTA:** See all fall colors

The close ("your favorite styles are still available... we just added a few more options") tells her directly that nothing she relies on is going away.
