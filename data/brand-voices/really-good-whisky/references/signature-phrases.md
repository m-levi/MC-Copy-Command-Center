# RGW — Signature Phrases

Approved vocabulary and phrase bank for The Really Good Whisky Company. These are the turns of phrase that make a send unmistakably RGW. Use them naturally — never force them, never pile them on. One or two per email is the right dose.

---

## Signature Openers

- **"We've just secured something rather special."** — Limited release / new arrival. The defining brand line. Use sparingly; it loses power when overused.
- **"Spring has arrived, and so has something rather lovely."** — Seasonal / parallel construction. Adaptable: "Autumn is here, and so has…"
- **"Your cabinet has been waiting."** — Re-engagement. Playful personification. Works as a subject line or an opener.
- **"Your cabinet deserves better."** — Aspirational push to upgrade collection. Use sparingly.
- **"Let's talk about peat."** (or cask finishes, or regions) — Educational opener. Direct, confident.
- **"Ever wondered what makes a bottle truly special?"** — Educational / relatable hook.

## Signature Middles

- **"The kind of whisky that makes guests pause mid-conversation."** — Emotional selling, sophisticated context.
- **"This isn't your everyday dram."** — Reinforces premium positioning.
- **"A whisper of smoke that lingers beautifully."** — Template for sensory detail with restraint.
- **"We treat each bottle like liquid gold."** — Shipping / handling / post-purchase. Signature care line.
- **"Hand-picked. Expertly chosen. Delivered with care."** — Three-fragment hero line. Use as closer or mid-email rhythmic beat.
- **"From our London showroom to your door."** — Global-reach framing.

## Signature Closers

- **"Questions? Drop us a line. We're passionate about this stuff."** — Warm close. Reinforces approachability.
- **"Our team is always here to guide you to your perfect pour."** — Service-forward close.
- **"Need a recommendation? Just ask."** — Short, confident close. Often follows a product send.
- **"Cheers, The Really Good Whisky Company Team"** — Every sign-off, without exception. Never an individual name.

## Phrase Bank — Product & Quality

- **"Exceptional whisky"** — replaces "amazing whiskies." The default quality descriptor.
- **"Hand-picked selection"** / **"Expertly curated"** — selection process language.
- **"Rare, limited-edition bottles"** — exclusivity framing (note Oxford comma).
- **"Dream drams lie within"** — browsing / discovery contexts. Use sparingly (once per quarter, not once per week).
- **"Something brilliant"** — understated quality descriptor. British sensibility.
- **"Beautifully complex"** / **"Elegantly structured"** — tasting-note vocabulary.
- **"Expression"** — alternative to "bottle" when referring to a specific whisky ("this Islay expression").

## Phrase Bank — Community & Membership

- **"Really Good Club members"** — never "members" alone in first mention; always specify the club by name.
- **"First dibs"** / **"First access"** — member benefit framing.
- **"24 hours before anyone else"** — specific, quantified exclusivity.
- **"Community of connoisseurs"** — community framing. Respectful of audience.
- **"The membership pays for itself in just two purchases."** — ROI line for club recruitment. Concrete, unsentimental.
- **"Insider access"** / **"Insider knowledge"** — for club-recruitment or member-exclusive sends.

## Phrase Bank — Shipping & Trust

- **"Fully insured, worldwide delivery."**
- **"Premium packaging. Complete protection."**
- **"Free shipping on UK orders over £150."** — exact phrasing for the threshold.
- **"Your exceptional whisky arrives safely, guaranteed."** — trust close.

## Phrase Bank — Urgency (natural only)

- **"These won't last the week."**
- **"Down to single digits."**
- **"We've secured 30 of them."** — specific numbers build credibility.
- **"One last heads-up on this one."** — last-call opener.
- **"Act fast."** — acceptable, but never stacked with other urgency cues.

---

## Phrase Frequency Rules

- Any one signature phrase: **no more than once per campaign month.** A reader who sees "rather special" in three consecutive sends stops reading "rather special" as anything.
- **"Dream drams lie within"** and **"Your cabinet deserves better"**: once per quarter at most. These are high-impact, low-frequency phrases.
- **"We treat each bottle like liquid gold"**: once per email, and not in every email — reserve for sends where shipping or care is actually the focus.
- **"Cheers, The Really Good Whisky Company Team"**: every send. The exception that proves the frequency rule.

---

## Do Not Use — Anti-Phrases

These are often _almost_ right but cross the line into wrong-brand:

- "Whiskies" — plural. Ever. Full stop.
- "Something super special" / "something really cool" — casualness breaks positioning.
- "Truly extraordinary" / "absolutely incredible" — adverbs overreach.
- "Utterly divine transcendent nectar" — parody of the brand's own voice.
- "Hey whisky lovers!" / "Fellow whisky nerds!" — register-breaking.
- "Treat yourself" / "Indulge yourself" — generic luxury clichés the brand rejects.
- "Don't miss out" — desperation.
- "Once-in-a-lifetime" — clichéd.
- "Elevate your collection" — banned word.
