# FatSwag — Pre-Send Checklist

Run this before a send goes out. If any item fails, fix before submitting. Treat it as a real checklist — not a vibes pass.

---

## The 12 Checks

1. **3-second comprehension.** Could this email be understood in under 3 seconds? If not, cut copy.
2. **Headline length.** Is the headline 5 words or fewer?
3. **Body copy restraint.** Does the email have more than one line of body copy beneath the headline? It probably shouldn't. Cut until it earns its space.
4. **No product riffing.** Did I riff on any product text or graphic (extending the joke, "playing on" the design)? Remove it immediately. The product speaks for itself.
5. **Read-aloud test.** Does anything sound corny read out loud? Kill it.
6. **CTA language.** Is the CTA drop-culture (SHOP THE DROP, COP YOURS, GET IT FIRST) — not generic (SHOP NOW, BUY NOW, EXPLORE)?
7. **Subject line length.** 6 words or fewer?
8. **Zero emojis.** Nowhere. Not in subject. Not in body. Not in CTA.
9. **Voice consistency.** Same energy top to bottom — no paragraph that drifts corporate or explanatory.
10. **Visual sibling check.** Would this look at home next to a Vans or GV Gallery email? If it feels louder, wordier, or more polished than those — pull it back.
11. **No brand explanation.** Does any line explain what FatSwag is or why it's different? Delete it. Swag doesn't position his brand, he runs it.
12. **Vocabulary check.** Is every slang word something Swag actually says? Cross-check `sms-vocabulary.md`. If it's not on the list, don't use it.

---

## Quick Kill List

If any of these appear, the email isn't ready:

- "Real" as a qualifier ("real streetwear," "real fit") — copywriter language
- "Elevate," "curated," "designed to," "game-changer," "must-have," "essential"
- Pity or sympathy framing ("We know it's hard to find clothes that fit...")
- Competitive language ("Nobody else is doing this")
- Fake urgency ("HURRY!" "LAST CHANCE!") — the urgency is real; state the fact
- Any paragraph longer than one line in a campaign email
- Any emoji, anywhere

---

## Tone-Shift Safety Checks

- **Welcome flow:** first-person from Swag is allowed here. Still action-oriented, not storytelling. Sign-off: `— Swag`.
- **Post-purchase:** gratitude, not pitch. Don't upsell in an order confirmation.
- **Sale/promo:** the time window is the story. The discount is just the number.
- **VIP drops:** "before everyone else" frame, quieter than a broadcast drop.
